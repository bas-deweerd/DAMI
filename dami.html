Introduction

This course is based on the idea that learning is doing is learning.

You will make plenty exercises and demonstrate your understanding of your solutions through the notebook format we will be using. Notebooks are a great way to conduct reproducible research and in a sense you will be doing research. You will integrally explain, annotate, and comment on your code in a notebook.

It is commonly known that 70-80% of data research consists of data preparation. You will therefore spend ample time to develop your data preparation skills.

Machine learning is a huge and complex topic. In this course you will scratch the surface of supervised learning, unsupervised learning and some deep learning. But rest assured, after you've finished this course you will have a much better understanding of machine learning than most people merely talking about it.

Your teacher does not qualify as a machine learning expert. Not even near. In fact he is a student and casual user of it himself. You, being 4th year students, however, are expected to be able to learn from other sources than just your teacher. Just as you would not depend too much on a senior colleague after graduation. In fact, being able to train and skill yourself by selecting proper learning sources and setting up your personal training plan, is the single most important quality of a knowledge worker in today's professional world. In this course you will be guided and facilitated to upskill yourself in the field of practical data analysis and machine learning. And rest assured: there are many resources available on the web to learn Machine Learning from. Some of these sources have been mentioned in the additional material list below. There are many, many more learning sources available, depending on your personal interest. Do not forget to put evidence of chasing your personal machine learning interests in your portfolio!

Finally a word of warning. Applying machine learning is only a small part in the process of data mining, or data analysis if you like. There are several process methodologies defining the data mining process. CRISP-DM (cross-industry process for data mining) is probably the most well-known and accepted in industry:

Here you can find a brief description of the CRISP-DM process. This course is mainly focusing on the modelling stage of the process and on part of the data preparation stage. If we zoom in on the modelling process, we can define another (sub)process:

Here, you can read a brief introduction to this modeling process.

Be aware that the Data Preparation stage in CRISP-DM entails much more the just importing, tidying and transforming data as defined in the modeling sub process. In a large organization getting data from various enterprise level systems and consolidating and normalizing it in order to be able to start modeling it, can be a very complex and laborious process.

You should always keep in mind that the data mining process entails much more than just modeling the data in order to predict. If you want to successfully apply data mining, or data analysis, you have to spend much time and effort to the organizational embedding of the problem you are trying to solve. This is were the other stages of the CRISP-DM process are all about.

Final Project

  • Conduct a data analysis of a problem (with accompanying dataset) of your own interest
  • Report on your analysis and reflection in a Jupyter Notebook:
  • what is the (business) problem you want to solve or want to learn about?
  • how is this problem data related?
  • what data do you have at your disposal and what do you want to extract from it?
  • for each step in your analysis
    • what are you aiming at with this step?
    • why this approach?
    • how should you interpret the result of your step?
  • how does the acquired information (from the data) contribute to solving your original problem?
  • Reflect on what you've learned in this project and in the entire course.
  • Blend all exercise notebooks and your final report notebook in a portfolio and submit for assessment. Your portfolio is a zipped folder containing all your notebooks.

Assessment and grading

Each week you will deliver a notebook in which you investigated that week's topic through a set of exercises. Over the last weeks of this course you will be working on a final assignment. Within the boundaries of this course you are free to choose the subject of your final assignment. You are advised to do your final assignment as a couple. If you can't interest any of your classmates to join you in researching a topic of your interest, you are allowed to work alone.

You will present the results of your final assignment to your classmates and possibly other interested persons.

The notebooks of the weekly assignments and your final assignment comprise your portfolio for this subject. Your final assignment will be assessed on soundness of research and reasoning and on depth of analysis.

Your portfolio, timely delivery of the various assignments) and your performance in a brief oral assessment will determine your grade for this subject.
